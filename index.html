Little Head Starters

    Welcome to Little Head Starters.

    Children readily absorb information in the early years of their lives.

    Little Head Starters is a structured, fun, weekly program providing the opportunity for young children to be exposed to literacy and numeracy skills before they attend school.

    Learning is play-based through songs, games and activities.

    This parent/child interactive program for 2-6 year olds focuses on:

    • Alphabet recognition & phonics (sight words for advanced kids)
    • Number recognition & counting (simple mathematics for advanced)
    • Colours, Shapes
    • Clock, Calendar
    • Memory enhancement
    • Social skills, eg sharing, taking turns and show and tell
    • Fine motor and gross motor skills

    The program structure consists of:

    Group time:
    We begin with a greeting song where each child suggests an action for the group to follow.
    We look at the calendar and learn through songs about the days of the week, months and seasons.
    During ‘Mail Time’, each child receives a letter with their name on it (name recognition) and the letter of the day is revealed and its corresponding sound is practised. A mini show & tell with objects beginning with that sound follows to help develop the child’s confidence skills.

    The number of the day is presented and we sing songs and play games with numbers.
    We look at the time on our clock and investigate the shape of the day.

    Table top activities
    These activities help to develop fine motor skills, imagination and reinforce the theme. Activities include craft, play dough, tracing/colouring, working with pegs, and threading.

    Body coordination games
    Games which include jumping, crawling, throwing and catching balls, etc help to develop gross motor skills and spatial awareness.

    Story time
    Language and concepts continue to develop during story time. Questions are posed to encourage children to think and express ideas.
    The program includes many activities to engage and challenge your child. It provides the opportunity for you and your child to spend quality learning time together as well as time to interact with others.

    Parent participation ensures that every child receives one-on-one attention whilst attending the session and can take home ideas and skills to continue learning at home. It is extremely rewarding to be actively involved in your child's development.
